Numeral 30    Bing Gui

Hexagram: Fire on Mountain

Heavenly Morning Star

Hour Pillar Gui Chou – Water Ox Gui Mao – Water Rabbit
Metaphor Willow on the riverside in spring. Fishing in the cold pond.
Explanation You come across good luck in your early years, but you need to be firm in your ambitions. Manage your expectations as to what the rewards might be.

Heavenly Revolutionary Star

Hour Pillar Gui Si – Water Snake Gui Wei – Water Goat
Metaphor Hungry bird flying towards the forest. Fishing with a broken net.
Explanation Although your circumstances are humble you have some support. You have high hopes and you need to manage your expectations.

Heavenly Phoenix Star

Hour Pillar Gui You – Water Rooster Gui Hai – Water Pig
Metaphor Watching the moon from the pavilion. Moss receiving rain.
Explanation You can expect a satisfactory result. Your business is small but makes a lot of profits.

 

Destiny:
In this life you have to go through a storm of wind and waves, ford the river and go through the mountain. Generous and true to yourself, you know how to enjoy life and you know how to go about getting things done. Yours is a straightforward character that is quite frank.  Because of your nature may have to experience a loss of money from doing what is right.  You do the right thing but it leads to the wrong result. Therefore, beware of attracting lawsuits. Your adopted son is really like a son to you. Siblings treat you as a stranger, your friends know you best.  If you really want everything you have to distinguish the snake from the dragon. You need to know how to spot virtuous people and how to spot the ignoble ones.

 

Luck Verses

Foundation: 
Don’t expend extra energy to climb a high mountain.
The old trees with very deep roots reach upwards to heaven.
Ox and Dog years bring special celebrations.
Someone by the waterside or with a water connection,
Helps you establish a family and gain a fortune.

Life Path:
If your heart is merciful, in the end you’ll be peaceful.
When the wind dies down, get your fishing rod ready.
Establish yourself by leaving for overseas,
Where you can catch a big fish alone with ease.

Marriage:
Romance will not blossom with the boy or girl next door.
A thousand miles away the moon is bright.
Go far from home to meet the one who’s right.
Say no to wild romances or empty flings for a night.

Children:
Flowers outside the wall have all withered,
Leaving only one fruit that is half green, half yellow.
Among brothers from all the four seas and five lakes, a good fellow
Who appreciates coming to the jade hall, offers you a son, for adoption.

Harvest:
When you meet Rabbit or Rooster it won’t be too unlucky,
When you ride the Dragon or Horse there is worry.
Prepare to adapt to changes with people and industry,
The road is bumpy, there will be some difficulty

 

Style: Wild Goose Soaring in the Sky
You get rich not because of your ancestors.
Your success does not depend on your relatives.
You will get beautiful jade from digging the mountain.
You will get a lot of gold from plowing the field.
With children there are feelings of kindness and complaint.
With your spouse you do not share the same philosophy.
When you have fame you attract ten thousand admirers.
And you will meet bosom friends a thousand miles away.
