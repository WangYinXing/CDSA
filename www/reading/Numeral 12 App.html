Numeral 12 Yi Yi

Hexagram: Wind on Wind

Stars

Heavenly Witch Star

Year or Hour Pillar Yi Chou – Wood Ox Yi Mao – Wood Rabbit 
Metaphor Withering lotus leaf in the rain. Duckweed floating in a lot of water.  
Explanation Be wary of other people when the going gets tough. Wandering about throughout your life.

Heavenly Poison Star

Year or Hour Pillar Yi Si – Wood Snake Yi Wei – Wood Goat 
Metaphor Setting sun on the horizon. Decayed grass in January endures frost after frost.
Explanation Good luck in your final years. Be cautious of untoward events in your later years.

Purple Morning Star

Year or Hour Pillar Yi You – Wood Rooster Yi Hai – Wood Pig
Metaphor Wonderful sword transforms into a dragon. Pearls and jade buried in the mud.
Explanation In your unlucky days there will be changes and eventually you can fly. Even if others do not appreciate your talents, stay put, do things on your own, value yourself.

Poetic Life Reading

Destiny:

This life has a lot of regrets, but if you take it easy you can enjoy leisure. When you want to walk, fate tells you to stand still.  When you want to come inside, fate tells you to go away.  Destiny opposes your wishes, nothing is smooth.  After advancing you retreat, one step forwards, two steps back. You like to learn a lot, but your understanding is not profound.  In the beginning you are as fierce as a tiger, in the end you are quite motionless, like a dead snake curled on the floor. Your enthusiasm for things does not last and you do not always finish what you start. Do not ask for too many favors, and do not take on board other’s criticism. Whatever appears to be malevolent turns out to be not that nasty.  Whatever appears to be fortunate turns out to be not that promising.

 

Luck Verses

Foundation: 
On meeting noble people, they will point out the path you should be taking.
You are a person who is most upstanding.
During Tiger or Dragon years good friends you will make,                                         
In Dog and Goat years opportunities turn up for you to take.

Life Path:
You have to walk a thousand miles.
Even if exhausted, you have to go on,
To find the job or take the exam.
Wait until you can ride the horse to see the results,
Ox and Goat years will bring good rewards.

Marriage:
Like mandarin ducks you make good companions.
Even though your characters are not so similar,
Able to overcome difficulties together,
Enduring biting winds and cold frosts bitter.

Children:
The poor grass is under attack,
From wild wind and heavy rain.
Yet there are two fruits and flowers.
Youngest son is the one who is clever.

Harvest:
As you build up a career, not much support from friends.
Spring is over, yet you cannot stay until the banquet ends.
You look back to see the setting sun, the clouds are beautiful, captivating.
Spend your final days like the lonely goose flying in the west wind.

 

Style: Goose Flying over Beautiful Marsh
When you are enjoying good luck, beware of bad luck coming suddenly.
You are as upright as a pine tree.
The clouds will hide behind mountains at night.
Green willow buds will give rise to a new branch.
What you built originally will form a good basis for taking hold of new opportunities.
In the autumn, a pair of wild geese fly together.
In the end, on a sand dune, a lone mandarin duck without a companion.
Thunder arouses the Dragon and the Snake.
Rain and dew, Ox and Goat, will come when the time is right. 
A good salary and acquiring property will happen in their own time.
Your character is like clear water.
During your younger years you are quite a romantic person, like the mandarin ducks playing in the water.
In old age, you can enjoy life, with lots of gold bars and plum blossoms.

  

Commentary

Destiny

By taking a relaxed approach, you can enjoy life. Do not let circumstances or other people’s disapproval put you off from pursuing your goals.

Luck Verses

Foundation

“Noble people” are helpful people you encounter when you need guidance. Tiger, Dragon, Dog and Goat years can be lucky times.

Life Path

“Wait until you can ride the horse” suggests that you need to wait for the right time. You might need to work hard until the time is right.

Marriage

You can find a good match in someone who has a different character to your own. “Enduring biting winds and cold frosts bitter” means you are able to get through hard times as a couple.

Children (‘Children’ can also mean subordinates or followers)

“Two fruits and some flowers” means you can have two sons (fruits) and some daughters (flowers). The “wild wind and heavy rain” suggests that having children is not straightforward. You can have many followers. The most capable followers come later.

Harvest

Spring refers to the time of your youth. To not be able to “stay until the banquet ends” suggests that you might not be able to make the most of all the opportunities that come your way. However, when you look back on your life “the clouds are beautiful and captivating” implies that you would be able to look back on many good times, even if you are on your own.

Style

“You are as upright as a pine tree,” means you have a honourable character. Beware of bad luck coming suddenly is suggested by “The clouds will hide behind mountains at night”. You can enjoy marital harmony in the mid-to-late years of your life. “Gold bars and plum blossoms” evoke an image of a wealthy and leisurely lifestyle.
