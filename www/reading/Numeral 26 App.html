Numeral 26    Bing Ji

Hexagram: Fire on Earth

Heavenly Beginning Star

Hour Pillar Ji Chou – Earth Ox Ji Mao – Wood Rabbit
Metaphor Digging for treasure and finding gold. Withering buds receiving rain.
Explanation Whatever you want you get as you wish. Everything is prosperous. You meet help in times of difficulty.

Heavenly Terrace Star

Hour Pillar Ji Si – Earth Snake Ji Wei – Earth Goat
Metaphor Planting jade in the blue field. Cicadas singing in the willow tree in autumn.
Explanation Accumulating good deeds in the dark brings great fortune later. Whatever difficulties are gone and you are entering lucky times.

Heavenly Worry Star

Hour Pillar Ji You – Earth Rooster Ji Hai – Earth Pig
Metaphor Fierce tiger patrolling the mountain. Watching the sky at the bottom of a well.
Explanation You work hard to get ahead, but beware of the risks. It is a good idea to manage your expectations when you do not have all the information.

 

Destiny:
This life is half full and half empty. Sometimes it is clear, sometimes it is muddy.  When you are supposed to be popular, you are not really popular. When you are supposed to be lonely, you are not lonely. You are not a monk and you are not a Daoist priest. Somehow there is not quite enough to go round, it is like a frantic juggling act to keep all the balls in the air, or like moving nine lids around to cover ten pots. When it comes to expressing your ideas, what you lack in being articulate, you compensate for by loving being flamboyant. You bury your ambition and you betray your intelligence. Since you cannot make up your mind, you experience both success and failure. It is suitable for you to leave your hometown and live in another place.  If you were born in the south, then in your later years you will be living in the north. If there is no danger and no obstruction, even the hidden diseases cannot be perilous. Your future meets with wind and frost quite early in your life. It is during those days that you build up the foundation for your success.

 

Luck Verses

Foundation: 
Leave your hometown, north and south you travel,
Build up career, from east to west you bustle.
Far from home, get to know places powerful.
Alas, fame and fortune are only occasional.

Life Path:
Peach trees in your garden are not yet blossoming,
One branch of the plum tree blossoms in the east wind.
Even though snow and frost have long been governing,
In the end, your house receives warm sun coming in.

Marriage:
Like the flowers of water plants that float around,
Unattached, you remain single for many a season.
Enjoying good times when you have a companion.
Cuckoo crying over azalea, you might end up forlorn.

Children:
A flower blooms, you have a daughter,
By the west wind carried, she goes to get married.
Bamboo grows well, many grandchildren thereafter,
Not like dragons, more like worms, not really go-getters.

Harvest:
There is a rabbit, you see the side of it.
Shoot an arrow, good tidings are expected.
You are invincible, you got what you wanted.
Hunting dogs bark at bushes, rabbit has sprinted.

 

Style: Lone Wild Goose Deserted by the Flock
You watch the steps from afar.
When you really climb the ladder it is very exhausting.
You spend three springs in the snow, to realize the different faces of people.
Like a crowd watching a chess match, they cheer you on when you win, they criticize you when you lose.  You are treated differently according to your achievement.
The wild goose flying south loses its companion.
Outside the forest you pick up a bud in spring.
If you still work hard for a better future, you will have more than enough for your needs then you can fill your golden cup with good wine.
