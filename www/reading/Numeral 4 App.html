Numeral 4    Jia Ding

 

Hexagram: Thunder on Earth

 



Heavenly Obstacle Star

Hour Pillar Ding Chou – Fire Ox Ding Mao – Fire Rabbit
Metaphor Duckweed floating on the stream in the rain. Faded chrysanthemum in the frost.
Explanation Make the most of the good times for you do not know what is round the next corner. Fortunately, you will pull through any crisis in your later years.

Heavenly Witch Star 

Hour Pillar Ding Si – Fire Snake Ding Wei – Fire Goat
Metaphor Peony in a golden vase. Frog making a noise in the urn.
Explanation Be aware that fame and fortune can be changeable. You have big ambitions, yet you need to make them happen.

Heavenly Illusion Star

Hour Pillar Ding You – Fire Rooster Ding Hai – Fire Pig
Metaphor Finding the clouds in the blue sky. Enjoying the cool breeze beside the pond.
Explanation You need to seek support for your endeavors. Although your plans can be successful, be prepared to overcome obstacles.

 

Destiny:

You are a handsome person, with clear eyebrows and bright eyes. You have a noble character, which is well disciplined and follows the rules.  Although you have a wide knowledge of many subjects, your understanding lacks depth.  Perceptive of your own position, you know how to distinguish between noble and ordinary people. Full of great ambitions, be careful not to lose your way. When handling large amounts of money you are too generous, yet with small amounts you are too tight-fisted, giving a somewhat unbalanced outlook. Forget the past, lose the future, you know what to do. With good experience you can turn misfortune into fortune. Even with bad luck you will eventually find good fortune and wealth. 

Luck Verses

Foundation:
An inheritance scattered far and wide,
Crowing rooster on the birch tree.
Success or failure what will it be?
It’s hard to say, enjoy each day.

Life Path:
When meeting misfortune, cry not over spilt water.
There’s no need to confront problems, let them be.
Leave behind the hustle and bustle of the city.
Like a dragon in the wilderness, no need to compete.

Marriage:
Startled mandarin ducks swim apart in the pond.
Laughing at the tree blossoms dancing in the wind,
Happy in the Tiger’s den and the Dragon’s spring,
Never mind what the Rooster and Dog may bring.

Children:
One branch of peach blossom in the wind.
Peach fruits will at most be three.
Young phoenix of another kind,
Grow under bamboo, to fly in time.

Harvest:
On meeting the Snake, do not ask where to find the road to happiness.
On meeting the Tiger, beware of Rat and Rooster.
You notice other people’s hearty laughter.
Aware you hear the rooster crow as the moon sets.

 

Style: Nightingale Singing in Warm Spring
An inheritance scattered here and there, 
Chickens cluck in the shadow of the tree.
The baboons call out under a cold moon over a mountain that’s empty.
In the forest there are winds, so high, the birds are far too tired to fly.
Ducks and geese soar through the sky, flocks flying in formation.
Mandarin ducks hit by winds, have to find their own way.
Business brings dispute and gossip.
The roots and the veins are set up.
In this world, what is meant to be is happening.
There is the unlimited beauty of nature,
The plum blossoms in the snowy, early spring.
