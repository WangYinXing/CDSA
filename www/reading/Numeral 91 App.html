Numeral 91    Gui Jia

Hexagram:  Mountain on Thunder

Stars

Heavenly Gloomy Star

Hour Pillar Jia Zi – Wood Rat Jia Yin – Wood Tiger
Metaphor Green pine and bamboo. Appreciating the moon from the pavilion.
Explanation In late years, peaceful and happy. Things are getting better all the time with help from others.

Heavenly Dragon Star

Hour Pillar Jia Chen – Wood Dragon Jia Wu – Wood Horse
Metaphor Autumn chrysanthemums and spring orchids. Riding on the rocky path.
Explanation Seeking fame and fortune, everything is to your satisfaction. After overcoming all the difficulties and danger you are in a safe and stable place.

Heavenly Calamity Star

Hour Pillar Jia Shen – Wood Monkey Jia Xu – Wood Dog
Metaphor Facing the moon clad with clouds. Gold hidden within the pages of a book.
Explanation Be content and there will be no worries. Accumulated wealth is sparse, so do not expect it to last long.

Poetic Life Reading

Destiny:

This is a person who is understanding and open-minded. Your attitude towards doing things involves taking care of the all the details. When you are patient you are like a sleeping Buddha. When you are impatient you are like fighting Buddha holding up fists. Within your character there is both rigidity and flexibility, yet you will not kneel down to flatter others.  Gossip is like a rope that binds your feet together. Fame and fortune can imprison your being.  Between you and your children there is much room for improvement when it comes to understanding each other. The relationship you have with your relatives has gone sour. In the morning you need to think and in the evening you need to calculate, so you only sleep for a few hours each night. Even though you try all means, only one out of three decades turns out to be fortunate. Having tasted all the harsh wind and bitter frost early in life, you can then enjoy the leisurely life.

 

Luck Verses

Foundation:
Young swallows, when wings fully grown, leave the nest,
To go to grand mansion with carved pillars, the best.
Riding the wind and dancing to heaven,
Lead the way across distant horizon,
On a lengthy journey of miles ten thousand.

Life Path:
In Ox year set off on the road to succeed.
In Rat year, benevolent boss you will meet.
Under his care, you will establish a great career.
Hold a bow and arrow to chase after the deer.
The deer is your goal, but everything is virtual.

Marriage:
Lonely in spring, outside window full moon shines through.
Want to play a joyful melody, yet you are playing the blues.
Away to a distant marsh, mandarin ducks flying.
Watch other water fowls in the pond playing.

Children:
In wind and rain, peach blossom petals falling.
Did not imagine some of them would be going.
Who believes, in back garden, flowers again blooming,
On a branch, one wonderful fruit hanging.

Harvest:
To look at gold and pearls around you is not satisfying.
Needless to mention it depends on luck and good timing.
Bell chimes, water clock has dripped to dawn, no dreaming.
Cuckoo is telling you that the evening clouds are arriving.

 

Style: Beautiful Moon on an Autumn Night
Moonbeam shines over a thousand miles, cool and clear.
Lonely goose calls in the middle of night to seek companion.
Parents are gone like clouds leaving you behind.
Siblings are like rivers flowing to the east without return.
You live a happy and stable life in later years.
In the beginning you have to run about and the achievement was not satisfactory.
Don’t laugh at the beautiful scenery of spring.
Listen to the nightingale under the peony.    

Commentary

Destiny

Although life often seems complicated, you can choose not to think too much about things. “Having tasted all the harsh wind and bitter frost early in life,” means you deal with difficulties when you are young. Your luck improves later on in life.

Luck Verses

Foundation

You leave home as soon as you are ready to be independent. After a long journey (“journey of miles ten thousand”) you reach a good destination (“grand mansion with carved pillars”).

Life Path

“Hold a bow and arrow to chase after the deer” implies that you are dedicated to pursuing your goals.

Marriage

Love and marriage will not happen when you are young (“lonely in spring”). “Away to distant marsh, mandarin ducks flying” suggests that you will find a match later in life.

Children (‘Children’ can also mean subordinates or followers)

Even though you face difficulties (“wind and rain”), at first, there will be children (“flowers blooming”). One of your children will be amazing (“one wonderful fruit hanging”). For followers, there is one who is outstanding.

Harvest

There is more to life than material wealth (“To look at gold and pearls around you is not satisfying”). You prosper when you have good luck. Towards the end of your days you reflect on what you have been through, and gain insights on the ups and downs in your life.

Style

It’s important for you to find a spouse. Do not expect to rely on your relatives. “Don’t laugh at the beautiful scenery of spring. Listen to the nightingale under the peony,” suggests that you make the most of your youth.
