Numeral 14    Yi Ding

Hexagram: Wind on Earth


Heavenly Arrogance Star

Hour Pillar Ding Chou – Fire Ox Ding Mao – Fire Rabbit
Metaphor Fishing in deep lake. Withered grass is welcoming spring.
Explanation Your hard work will pay off and eventually you will get the rewards. Having been exhausted for a long-time, the tide has now turned and good times are ahead.

Heavenly Abundance Star 

Hour Pillar Ding Si – Fire Snake Ding Wei – Fire Goat
Metaphor Firefly coming from decayed grass. Spring orchid and autumn chrysanthemums.
Explanation Not very ambitious, yet you can be proud of yourself. Life will run a smooth course from early to late years.

Heavenly Lonesome Star

Hour Pillar Ding You – Fire Rooster Ding Hai – Fire Pig
Metaphor Deep valley woodcutter. Rat in the barn.
Explanation If you work hard you can make great gains. You will experience abundance.

 

Destiny:
This is a life ruled by a star with a good reputation that exceeds its talents. The stars are not that bright. When you are alone you feel comfortable.  There is no easy fortune in this life, you have to work for it. You cannot rely on your ancestors. Relatives are cold towards you. Few people can understand you but you can understand others.  Fortunately, the Heavenly Abundance Star is in power. Whatever appears risky is not dangerous. Whatever appears disastrous is not really so bad. You think about things a lot but do very little.  In your spare time, you are often found in contemplation.  When you have more money, you have less enjoyment.   It is good to develop stillness and quiet at home, for then you can live a long life.


Luck Verses

Foundation:
Not really much closeness with your good family.
When you go out in the world to make money,
There will be help, not from relatives, but from old friends.
Fame and fortune will come to you in the end.

Life Path: 
Tiger year is a good time for starting something new.
Remember to invite old friends to come visit you. 
You are like big bird Peng that flies far.
Great ambition brings the best reward.

Marriage:
It is all pre-destined, marriage can be short or long.
Under the new moon, when the flowers are all gone,
You are listening to crows.
In later years, when you are old,
You are not without a companion.

Children:
The fruits in your garden are not good.
Like a peach on a willow tree grown,
Adopt a child and treat as your own.
When you are old, grow bamboo,
Your students will be close to you.

Harvest:
Flowers are withering, nightingale is old,
Riding a crane through blue skies foretold.
Whatever happened before is like a dream.
Stroll along the garden path, gently roam.

 

Style: Fish Swimming in Clear Lake
Not much connection or contact with relatives.
Leaving your hometown, you have your own family elsewhere.
Success and failure all pre-destined.
Fame and fortune is unpredictable.
Wind disturbs the bees by the water.
When the water gets cold, mandarin ducks swim in own directions to catch fish.
Whether the flowers can produce fruits? Ask heaven.
It is difficult to get hold of your foundations.
Whether your friends are your true friends, you will have to test.
