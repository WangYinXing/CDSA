Numeral 93    Gui Bing

Hexagram:  Mountain on Fire

Stars

Treasury Star

Hour Pillar Bing Zi – Fire Rat Bing Yin – Fire Tiger
Metaphor Playing music in big earthenware container. Cold dew in lotus pond house.
Explanation Despite a humble background, there are people who appreciate your talents. Because you are wealthy there are no worries, you can enjoy an affluent lifestyle.

Stone Gate Star

Hour Pillar Bing Chen – Fire Dragon Bing Wu – Fire Horse
Metaphor Flowers wither on the side of rocks. Petals flying in the wind.
Explanation Be wary of the ancestral business approaching bankruptcy. Difficult to have high flying career. Be content with reasonable wishes.

Heavenly Prosperity Star

Hour Pillar Bing Shen– Fire Monkey Bing Xu – Fire Dog
Metaphor Moon is seen to wear clouds as clothes. Riding a horse with flowers on the street.
Explanation Wait quietly for time to come, result will be excellent. Much prosperity, there is nothing going against you.

Poetic Life Reading

Destiny:

This person likes to offer opinions on everything. Being articulate, you can make use of your good speaking skills.  You travel a lot, moving about from north to south, then south to north throughout your life. When you are in a hurry you can invite gossip. Love can turn into hate in your life. Brothers and sisters have different ideas to you. Friends cannot be too close to you. When you try to cover up your true feelings, you expose the reality. Do not pretend to be friendly with people who will not be your friends. There are days when your luck has yet to come. When the green dragon goes into the sea, then your name will be spread far and wide like thunder over the plain. This is when good luck comes to you.

At age forty-five look back to when you started your career,

Life Path:
A fortunate and leisurely life, there’s no worry,

Marriage:

Children:
Three branches, three flowers, in the clouds, blossoming.
At night wind and rain are coming.

Good wind brings you many glad tidings.

Harvest:
Ding and Ren years do not travel by boat.
Water Rooster year, something to worry about.
For sixteen years, many minor hassles.
Under the stars at night go fishing.

 

Style: Golden Bridge Getting Famous
You are a high ranking officer who can visit the emperor’s palace.

When you have luck, you will meet with happy events.

Commentary

Destiny

“When the green dragon goes into the sea, then your name will be spread like thunder over the plain,” has the connotation that you will become renowned, when the time is right.

Luck Verses

Foundation

“Classy mansion with famous garden, full of the air in spring” suggests that you have the good luck to live in a fabulous residence. “Outside the door peach and plum laughing in spring wind” means that you are in demand as a mentor with many who wish to study with you. In China there was the system of imperial exams, which was used for selecting government officials. Those who got the highest marks would get to meet the emperor. “Passed exams went and saw the emperor,” implies that you gained top qualifications, which led to a good start in your career.

Life Path

“Moving to a new home, there’s a boat to carry,” suggests that you move with a lot of possessions. In ancient China this would happen if you were an official in the empire being sent to do a job in another location and the government funded your move. “From a double career, you get double glory,” implies that you succeed in different roles. In a modern context this could be the description of a life of an expatriate who changes roles.

Marriage

A close and happy relationship is predicted. As a couple, you enjoy each other’s company (“Not noticing other waterfowls and not socializing,”). Romance and domestic bliss continues in your late years (“swallow gracefully dancing”).

Children (‘Children’ can also mean subordinates or followers)

Children and grandchildren are predicted. Your descendants bring you much happiness (“Good wind brings you many glad tidings”). For followers, you can expect many loyal ones.

Harvest

“Under the stars at night go fishing,” suggests that you take it easy (“go fishing”) late in life (“at night”).

Style

“You are a high ranking officer who can visit the emperor’s palace,” means you are someone who can rise through the ranks and get close to the source of power. This suggests that you might be at a senior level in a company, close to the CEO, or you might have a senior role in the government or a large organisation.
