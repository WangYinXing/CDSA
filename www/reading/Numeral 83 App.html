Numeral 83    Ren Bing

Hexagram:  Water on Fire

Stars

Heavenly Chef Star

Hour Pillar Bing Zi – Fire Rat Bing Yin – Fire Tiger
Metaphor Starting the journey on a snowy day. Autumn leaves flying in the wind.
Explanation Born into a modest background but with a strong determination, eventually there is success. When you are facing hardship there is a noble person giving you a hand.

Heavenly Pillar Star

Hour Pillar Bing Chen – Fire Dragon Bing Wu – Fire Horse
Metaphor Playing a flute inside a cave. Peony in spring.
Explanation You can enjoy a leisurely life. With early prosperity you can walk up to the blue sky.

Heavenly Contentment Star

Hour Pillar Bing Shen– Fire Monkey Bing Xu – Fire Dog
Metaphor Firefly giving out light on a cold night. The flower coming out of a rotten branch.
Explanation Whatever you do you cannot boast about it in front of other people. After failure there is a chance.

Poetic Life Reading

Destiny:

You are born straightforward and quick in action. Although you are bold in taking up responsibility, you are never bold without careful planning. When others give you flattery, you should not give in to flowery words. You believe in always standing upright. Unfortunately, what you get in return are complaints and resentments.  In working hard for everything it can bring lawsuits and gossip.  Out of the kindness of your heart, you carry another person across the river in your boat. What you get in return are hassles and worries.

Commentary

Destiny

You believe in doing the right thing. Be aware that not all your good deeds will be appreciated.

Luck Verses

Foundation

“Carriage has turned over, so you have to put it upright again,” suggests that when things don’t go according to plan, you will fix any problems. “Good friends will come to bring you to the capital city,” implies that you will get support to reach the headquarters or centre of influence, which is not necessarily the capital.

Life Path

Due to the help from good people you can find success again and again. Being outdoors and appreciating nature (“spend time on mountains with water for your enjoyment”) is preferable to getting involved in short-lived, romantic entanglements (“swallows and nightingales, romance has time limitation”).

Marriage

A second marriage might not compare to your first (“Cannot play the right note with an impure string, it’s not like the old song, you’re used to playing”). There are good times (“bright moon in the cool breeze”), yet there are also grumbles (“Cries from crane and baboon sound like complaining”).

Children (‘Children’ can also mean subordinates or followers)

You might face challenges when it comes to having children (“After rain and wind not many flowers blooming”). It is predicted that you will eventually have three children. Some of your children turn out to be outstanding. For followers, you attract some who are mediocre and some who are highly talented.

Harvest

Rabbit or Rooster years are likely to bring challenges. “In late spring, moon sets to hide behind mountains,” suggests that when you are a young adult you face difficulties. Instead of feeling sorry for yourself, it would benefit you to adopt a philosophical attitude and to create good karma by being charitable.

Style

After handling troubles, you feel hopeful again (“After the rain, the mountains are beautiful. When the clouds fly away, the full moon is bright again,”). You will do worthwhile things and leave a legacy (“When the goose has flown to a distant mountain, flowers have gone leaving behind two fresh fruits”). “Tiger’s waist is hurt by a knife. The ox horn is replaced by the violin bow,” suggests you might die in glory. The ox horn was used to make receptacles for wine, like wine glasses. During a formal dinner, people drink from the ox horn then they watch musicians play their instruments with a bow (similar to a violin). The end of the dinner (“ox horn is replaced by the violin bow”) is a metaphor for the end of life, with a dramatic and glorious ending (“Tiger’s waist is hurt by a knife”).
