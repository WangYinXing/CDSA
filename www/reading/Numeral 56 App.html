Numeral 56    Ji Ji

Hexagram: Earth on Earth

Heavenly Retreating Star

Year or Hour Pillar Ji Chou – Earth Ox Ji Mao – Wood Rabbit
Metaphor After the rain, plant corn. Planting lotus on the rocks.
Explanation Work hard when you are young to enjoy a good life in your later years. Work hard caring for your children and grandchildren. Encourage them to be independent.

Heavenly Gloomy Star 

Year or Hour Pillar Ji Si – Earth Snake Ji Wei – Earth Goat
Metaphor Ornamental sword in the box. Bird resting in the wood in spring.
Explanation Cherish your great talents regardless of what goes on in the world. Expert in planning, therefore, making a living is easy.

Heavenly Begonia Star 

Year or Hour Pillar Ji You – Earth Rooster Ji Hai – Earth Pig
Metaphor Well grown bamboo on the mountain in winter. Plum blossom in the vase.
Explanation Happy without worries, living quite well in old age. Make the most of life being quite contained and modest.

 

Destiny:
This is a life that is well planned and full of preparation.  You always make an effort to prepare for future.  Whilst you speak frankly with a kind heart, your character is haughty and you don’t believe in making compromises.  If you are blamed for something you haven’t done, you do not like it. Yet, you will not take advantage of others.  When it comes to distinguishing between what is right what is wrong you can easily tell the difference.  Since your strengths can be applied to overcome your weaknesses, it is easy to have accomplishments in whatever you do. Even so, there is not enough help due to a lack of people working for you. Your spouse is not able to offer much assistance. Although you may have a lot of children, do not expect too much of them. Money can be wasted in seeking an unreal name. Wealth can be generated in a skillful way, but the money will be gone for no reason. Bubbles on the water surface are just like elusive flowers, they are not real.


Luck Verses

Foundation: 
Among the thorns grows a tall, big, strong tree.
To build a tower, lay the foundation gradually.
Nothing gained in ten years, didn’t work out right.
It’s time to go home whilst the moon is still bright.

Life Path:
To seek precious friends there is no need,
Good company comes to work with you naturally.
Scent of the plum blossom at night most enjoyable,
With a few bosom chums under the moon in the breeze,
Can talk about anything, it is all so agreeable.

Marriage:
In the sand dunes, mandarin ducks share good times together.
Water fowl play among white apple blossom and red wild flowers.
In the evening, the crows take flight,
You’re left alone to enjoy your dreams at night.

Children:
Many flowers bloom from the lotus,
Yet, only one fruit in the rain.
On the river you row the boat at night,
Hear the fisherman playing the flute on the riverside.

Harvest:
With a mind that is always busy,
In Dog and Rooster year, it’s time to come out,
Travel ten thousand miles of beautiful scenery.
Look out over the jade low wall of your balcony,
Enjoy retirement in a home of beauty and luxury.

 

Style: Breaking Stone to Find Jade
You hammer on a piece of rock, when it is broken you see the beautiful of piece jade inside.
It is a very strange sort of jade, it is like a withered pine tree keeping the bamboo company.
Wild goose flies alone.
Water fowls standing on one foot in the sand.
Everywhere there are spring flowers, yet in your garden there are autumn chrysanthemums.
What will happen when you leave this world?  You have a son-in-law and a grandchild around.
