Numeral 35    Ding Wu

Hexagram: Earth on Mountain

Stars

Heavenly Bank Star

Hour Pillar Wu Zi – Earth Rat Wu Yin – Earth Tiger
Metaphor Crane on a big pine tree. Small stream flowing into sea.
Explanation Nice and noble, very ambitious. Great ambition and deep thinking, therefore eventually you will be successful.

 

Heavenly Blasting Star

Hour Pillar Wu Chen – Earth Dragon Wu Wu – Earth Horse
Metaphor Playing the flute over the high pavilion. Pointing at iron and it becomes gold.
Explanation Enjoying leisurely times. Make an effort and you will get rich and move beyond humble circumstances.

 

Heavenly Chariot Star

Hour Pillar Wu Shen – Earth Monkey Wu Xu – Earth Dog
Metaphor Wait for the tide to sail your boat. Digging the well to get spring water.
Explanation Waiting for the right time to move, then everything will be smooth. After you work hard to manage your business, you will arrive at a beautiful place.

Poetic Life Reading

Destiny:

You are all on your own and you have to make an extra effort.  All your relatives can only offer you void assistance and your siblings cannot offer anything substantial.  Those who are truthful to you are rare.  Onlookers, to see how you fail, are plentiful.  You have to suffer a lot from the wind and frost, and to taste all the bitterness and worries.  When your luck has yet to come, whatever you do is in vain. When you are unprepared, you will meet a noble person, then sparks light up the cold ashes. The future will be bright. No need to long for home, wherever you find pleasant is your home.

 

Luck Verses

Foundation:
Background is a good family,
Maybe even famous ancestors.
The past is the past, it’s history.
Create own fortune is what matters,
Even ditches can become bright rivers.

Life Path:
In Dragon and Tiger years you will prosper,
Walking on the path made of jade and gold.
When you reflect on the past, tales retold,
Many good memories you picture.

Marriage:
Pear blossoms blow away in the wind and rain,
Relationships come and go, time and again.
In the west garden a lot of plum and peach trees,
Even though mountain is full of plants, in the end you’re lonely.

Children:
One daughter, two sons arrive easily.
A good son like an immortal peach
In a fragrant garden so pretty,
Across it blows spring breeze.

Harvest:
In Tiger and Monkey years it is time to go
Seek new ventures in the jade capital.
Meet the emperor in the year of the Rooster,
An important post assigned then you prosper.

 

Style: Peach and Plum Trees are Glorious
In the Yellow River there is water for you to sail.
However, the water does not flow smoothly.
Phoenix flies out to the red mountain. The cloud is obstructing its flight path.
Wild geese fly over the wilderness in autumn.
Rooster crowing and you won’t find it peaceful.
The crane on the pine tree is full of freedom.
When the flower withers the fruit appears, when what is beautiful disappears what is rewarding appears.
Take it easy, because in this life things come and go many times.

Commentary

Destiny

You have to face difficulties (“suffer a lot from the wind and frost”). Hope is revived (“sparks light up the ashes”) when you encounter a benefactor (“noble person”) and the future looks bright.

Luck Verses

Foundation

“Even ditches can become bright rivers” suggests that you can start from nothing and achieve brilliance.

Life Path

“Walking on the path made of jade and gold” means you can gain wealth. You can look back on many good times in your life.

Marriage

“In the west garden a lot of plum and peach trees,” implies you have plenty of romantic opportunities. “Even though mountain is full of plants, in the end you’re lonely” suggests that you might choose not to settle down.

Children (‘Children’ can also mean subordinates or followers)

“A good son like an immortal peach in a fragrant garden so pretty, across it blows spring breeze” suggests that you are lucky when it comes to children because you have an amazing son. For followers, you can easily attract good followers and some of them will be outstanding.

Harvest

“In Tiger and Monkey years it is time to go seek new ventures in the jade capital” implies that in Tiger years and Monkey years you can go to the headquarters of a large organisation. You can find success with the position you are offered after meeting the leader in a Rooster year.

Style

The Yellow river is a big river in China. “In the Yellow river there is water for you to sail” implies that there are opportunities for you to go far. “However, the water does not flow smoothly” suggests that you encounter obstacles. “Phoenix flies out to the red mountain. The cloud is obstructing its flight path” means you encounter challenges. “Wild geese fly over the wilderness in autumn” suggests that things change all the time. “Rooster crowing and you won’t find it peaceful. The crane on the pine tree is full of freedom” alludes to you wanting to escape from the world.
